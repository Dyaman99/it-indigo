Discover Lake Ouachita

Nestled within the Ouachita National Forest, Lake Ouachita is Arkansas’ largest and clearest lake. With 40,000 acres of pristine water and scenic shoreline, the park offers a perfect balance of adventure, relaxation, and natural beauty.

About Lake Ouachita State Park

1. Outdoor Recreation & Water Adventures

Lake Ouachita is a destination for year-round outdoor recreation. Visitors enjoy swimming, water skiing, scuba diving, kayaking, and boating across its expansive, crystal-clear waters. Anglers can fish for bream, crappie, catfish, striped bass, and largemouth bass in both open waters and quiet coves. Surrounded by forested hills and scenic trails, the park also offers hiking opportunities such as the Caddo Bend Trail, along with guided programs including eagle cruises, kayak tours, and interpretive hikes.

2. Camping & Cabin Accommodations

The park features 93 campsites, including Class AAA, Class D, and walk-in tent sites—many located directly along the waterfront. For visitors seeking added comfort, eight fully equipped cabins provide lake views, kitchens, and modern amenities. Four camper cabins offer a more affordable option within the campground. These cozy units sleep four and include heating and air conditioning, a screened porch, picnic table, grill, and access to nearby bathhouses. They are also dog-friendly, making them ideal for families traveling with pets.

3. Amenities & Nearby Attractions

Lake Ouachita State Park offers a variety of facilities to enhance your stay, including picnic areas, swimming beaches, a full-service marina with boat and kayak rentals, bait and supplies, and a visitor center with exhibits and a gift shop. Historic Three Sisters’ Springs is located within the park, adding a cultural and natural point of interest. Conveniently located near Hot Springs and Hot Springs Village, the park provides easy access to additional attractions while maintaining its peaceful, natural setting.
